##                                                             Reflection

As a result of conducting the research on AI-assisted software development, I came to a conclusion that the influence of Artificial Intelligence on software engineers' working process is tremendous.
In the period before starting my investigation, all my knowledge related to the potential of Artificial Intelligence was confined to the possibility to create certain pieces of computer code using those technologies or automate the corresponding programming procedures. 
Nevertheless, it turned out that these developments had become an integral part of software engineering involving various tasks such as debugging, testing, project management, DevOps, documentation, security analysis, and decision making.
More specifically, it was rather surprising to me to see how many different applications based on Artificial Intelligence were used by software engineers in their work.
For example, tools like GitHub Copilot, Google Gemini Code Assist, and Amazon Q Developer do not just produce pieces of code.
They are helpful for developers when understanding logic of their programming actions and locating errors in order to improve efficiency of their work.
What is more, I did not expect to find so much use of AI-based tools in DevOps and software automation.
On the other hand, doing research, I have become aware of certain risks and limitations of the application of AI in software engineering.
First of all, I have found out that the use of this type of solutions could cause an increased dependence on the work done by AI resulting in the loss of critical thinking abilities. 
Moreover, another potential danger associated with AI is its inability to provide accurate and reliable code. Therefore, I understood that despite the fact that AI can save our efforts and time, it was important to check its output before using it.
Furthermore, while analyzing the topic, I understood that the role of human skills remains crucial for software engineers.
In spite of the availability of various types of AI, creativity, decision making, problem solving, and other qualities are important.
In this case, software engineers need to use their skills to identify the requirements and make decisions related to projects.
At the same time, this assignment made me reassess my stance on the future of the software engineering career.
Before conducting research for this assignment, I assumed that one day Artificial Intelligence could replace programmers. 
But now, based on my findings, I believe that Artificial Intelligence can change, but not substitute, the software engineering profession.
Programmers who know how to work with AI tools and constantly develop their skills will always be needed in the labor market.
In any case, I believe that being in the age of Artificial Intelligence would mean constantly upgrading oneself and remaining open to changes.
Being a future software engineer, I think it is vital to get to know the inner workings of AI systems and acquire the skills needed to cooperate with them safely. 
Far from being scared of Artificial Intelligence, a software engineer should know how to utilize it as an auxiliary tool and solve issues quickly and easily.
